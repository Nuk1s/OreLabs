# Purpose

This document defines the fundamental gameplay systems that together create the OreLabs gameplay ecosystem.

Each system has a unique responsibility.

Systems should complement each other rather than compete for the player's attention.

---

# Design Philosophy

Every system must answer a unique gameplay question.

No system should exist only to increase numbers.

Every system should create new decisions, unlock new possibilities or deepen existing gameplay.

---

# Core Systems

## Strength

Purpose:

Represents the player's fundamental progression.

Strength is the foundation of the entire progression ecosystem.

It unlocks stronger Pickaxes and supports future progression systems.

---

## Mining

Purpose:

Transforms player progression into resources.

Mining is the primary active gameplay activity.

Better progression allows access to more valuable ores rather than simply increasing mining speed.

---

## Pickaxes

Purpose:

Control exploration and mining capability.

Each Pickaxe expands what the player is able to mine and where the player can progress.

---

## Backpack

Purpose:

Extends active gameplay sessions.

Backpacks increase efficiency by reducing unnecessary interruptions while preserving inventory management.

---

## Economy

Purpose:

Converts player activity into meaningful progression.

Coins, Gems and future currencies support different parts of the progression ecosystem.

Currencies should never compete for identical purposes.

---

## Pets

Purpose:

Specialize progression.

Pets provide unique bonuses that encourage different strategies and player builds.

They enhance progression rather than replace it.

---

## Rebirth

Purpose:

Marks the completion of one progression chapter and unlocks the next.

Rebirth provides permanent progression bonuses and expands the available game world.

---

## Progression Chapters

Purpose:

Divide the game into meaningful stages.

Every chapter introduces new locations, resources, equipment and opportunities.

Each chapter should feel like a natural expansion of the previous one.

---

## Future Systems

Examples include:

- Forge
- Titles
- Crafting
- Artifacts
- Enchantments
- Events

Future systems must integrate into the existing ecosystem instead of creating isolated gameplay loops.

---

# System Relationships

Every core system contributes to one connected progression ecosystem.

No system should become mandatory while making others irrelevant.

The value of the ecosystem comes from interaction between systems.

---

# System Principle

A great system does not replace another system.

It gives that system a new purpose.
