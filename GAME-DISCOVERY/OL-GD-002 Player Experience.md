# Purpose

This document defines how the player should feel throughout the entire journey.

It does not describe mechanics.

It describes the emotional experience every mechanic must create.

---

# Core Experience

The player should always feel that there is something meaningful waiting ahead.

Progress must create anticipation rather than repetition.

Every unlocked mechanic should expand the game instead of replacing previous gameplay.

---

# Emotional Journey

## Beginning

The player enters a simple and understandable world.

The first goals are obvious.

Learning should happen naturally through interaction.

The player immediately understands that the game contains much more than what is currently visible.

Primary emotions:

- Curiosity
- Simplicity
- Discovery

---

## Early Progression

The player begins unlocking systems one by one.

Every new mechanic answers a new question instead of increasing existing numbers.

The player starts making decisions rather than following a single path.

Primary emotions:

- Excitement
- Exploration
- Growth

---

## Mid Game

Multiple progression systems begin interacting.

The player starts creating personal strategies.

Different choices lead to different playstyles.

Optimization becomes rewarding.

Primary emotions:

- Ownership
- Experimentation
- Optimization

---

## Late Game

The player understands the entire ecosystem.

Mastery becomes more important than raw progression.

Players compete through knowledge, planning and efficient builds.

Primary emotions:

- Mastery
- Competition
- Identity

---

## End Game

There is no true ending.

Every update opens another meaningful direction.

Veteran players return because new possibilities appear, not because old progress became useless.

Primary emotions:

- Anticipation
- Long-term commitment
- Prestige

---

# Player Motivation

The player should continuously ask:

"What can I unlock next?"

"What happens if I try another strategy?"

"How can I become more efficient?"

"What have other players discovered?"

These questions are more valuable than simply asking:

"How many coins do I need?"

---

# Active vs AFK

Active gameplay should always feel more engaging and more rewarding.

AFK progression exists only to support moments when the player cannot actively play.

The best discoveries should always happen during active gameplay.

---

# Competition

Competition should reward knowledge rather than endless grinding.

Players who better understand the game's systems should naturally outperform players who simply repeat actions.

---

# Long-Term Retention

Players should continue returning because:

- new goals appear;
- new combinations become possible;
- new strategies emerge;
- updates deepen existing systems.

The game should feel larger with every update while remaining familiar.

---

# Success Definition

The player should describe the experience as:

"I always have something meaningful to work toward."

"I understand the game, but I still discover new things."

"I never feel lost, even when the game becomes deeper."

---

# Experience Principle

The game should continuously replace certainty with curiosity.

Every answer should naturally lead to another meaningful question.
