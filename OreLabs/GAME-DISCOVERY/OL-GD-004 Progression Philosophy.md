# Purpose

This document defines the philosophy behind player progression.

Progression is not the process of increasing numbers.

Progression is the process of unlocking new possibilities.

Every progression system must expand the player's experience instead of simply making previous actions faster.

---

# Core Philosophy

The player should always feel that progress opens another meaningful direction.

Progression is designed as an expanding network of interconnected systems rather than a straight line.

Each new system should increase player choice while remaining understandable.

---

# The Progression Layers

The first game is built around multiple progression layers.

Each layer answers a different player question.

## Strength

Question:

"How powerful have I become?"

Strength represents the player's overall growth.

It unlocks access to stronger equipment and future progression milestones.

---

## Pickaxes

Question:

"Where can I go?"

Pickaxes define exploration.

A better pickaxe is not only faster.

It grants access to new ores, new locations and new opportunities.

Different pickaxes should create different mining capabilities instead of only increasing mining speed.

---

## Backpack

Question:

"How much can I accomplish before returning?"

Backpacks increase efficiency by extending active gameplay sessions.

They reduce interruptions without removing the importance of inventory management.

---

## Pets

Question:

"How efficiently can I use my progression?"

Pets amplify existing progression.

They provide specialized bonuses instead of replacing player progression.

Different pet combinations should encourage different playstyles.

---

## Rebirth

Question:

"Am I ready to enter the next stage of the game?"

Rebirth is not a reset.

Rebirth is a progression gate.

The game is divided into multiple progression stages, each representing a complete chapter of the player's journey.

Within each stage, the player develops their character by increasing Strength, upgrading Pickaxes, expanding Backpack capacity, collecting Pets and exploring available content.

Eventually, progression reaches a point where further advancement is intentionally limited.

To continue growing, the player must perform a Rebirth.

A Rebirth permanently increases the player's overall progression by providing global bonuses, such as higher coin gain, strength growth and other long-term multipliers defined by future systems.

More importantly, every Rebirth unlocks the next progression stage.

New stages introduce:

- new locations;
- new ores;
- new pickaxes;
- new backpacks;
- new pets;
- new progression opportunities.

Each Rebirth transforms previous progression into a stronger foundation rather than replacing it.

The player never repeats the same journey.

They revisit familiar systems with greater power while discovering entirely new content.

Rebirth is the bridge between chapters, not the end of one.
---

## Future Systems

Every future progression system must answer a unique player question.

If a new mechanic duplicates the purpose of an existing system, it should be redesigned or rejected.

---

# Interconnected Progression

No progression system exists in isolation.

Every upgrade should naturally support multiple other systems.

Example:

Strength unlocks Pickaxes.

Pickaxes unlock new Ores.

Ores generate Coins and Gems.

Coins improve Equipment.

Gems improve Pets and future progression.

Pets improve overall efficiency.

Efficiency accelerates future progression.

The result is a connected progression ecosystem instead of isolated mechanics.

---

# Active Progression

The most valuable progression should always happen through active gameplay.

AFK systems may support progression, but they should never become the primary way to advance.

Players should return because playing is more rewarding than waiting.

---

# Long-Term Growth

Progression should evolve in layers.

Early game introduces the foundations.

Mid game combines systems.

Late game focuses on optimization, specialization and mastery.

Future updates should deepen existing progression instead of replacing it.

---

# Design Rules

Every new progression mechanic should satisfy all of the following:

- Introduce a new meaningful decision.
- Connect with existing systems.
- Expand player possibilities.
- Preserve previously earned progress.
- Encourage experimentation.

Mechanics that only increase numbers without changing player decisions should be avoided.

---

# Success Definition

A successful progression system makes players think:

"I finally unlocked something new."

instead of

"My numbers became bigger."

---

# Progression Principle

The purpose of progression is not to finish the game.

The purpose of progression is to continuously expand the game.

Every major progression stage should have a meaningful completion point.

Players should feel that they have mastered the current stage before unlocking the next one.

Rebirth exists to transform mastery into a new beginning.
