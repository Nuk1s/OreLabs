# OL-GD-005 Economy Discovery

---

# Purpose

The OreLabs economy exists to create meaningful progression rather than numerical accumulation.

Its purpose is not to make players collect increasingly larger numbers, but to continuously unlock new opportunities, mechanics and stages of gameplay.

Every play session should move the player closer to a meaningful milestone.

---

# Core Philosophy

## Continuous Progression

The economy should create a feeling of continuous progression, not continuous grinding.

Players should return because they are excited to unlock the next stage of the game—not because they are forced to endlessly repeat the same activity.

---

## Perfect Balance

The economy should never feel too easy.

The economy should never feel exhausting.

OreLabs always aims to keep players inside the **Zone of Progress**, where every session feels rewarding while the next objective remains meaningful.

The player should constantly think:

> "Just a little more, and I'll unlock something new."

---

## Meaningful Purchases

Every major purchase must unlock new gameplay possibilities.

Purchases should change the player's experience rather than simply increase numbers.

Examples include:

- Unlocking a new Pickaxe
- Unlocking a new Backpack
- Unlocking a new Rebirth stage
- Unlocking new Mining Areas
- Unlocking new Pet strategies

Progress should always feel like expansion instead of inflation.

---

## Always Have Goals

Players should simultaneously have goals on multiple time scales.

### Short-Term Goals (5–15 minutes)

Examples:

- Buy a stronger Dumbbell
- Upgrade Backpack
- Purchase the next Pickaxe
- Complete a mining run

### Mid-Term Goals (30–120 minutes)

Examples:

- Reach the next Rebirth
- Unlock a new Area
- Improve Pet setup
- Obtain stronger Equipment

### Long-Term Goals (Days or Weeks)

Examples:

- Complete Forge progression
- Collect rare Pets
- Reach Leaderboards
- Unlock future Worlds
- Complete Collections

Players should never ask:

> "What should I do next?"

The next objective should always be obvious.

# Resource Philosophy

## Coins

Primary progression currency.

Used for purchasing progression-related systems and upgrades.

---

## Gems

Premium gameplay currency.

Represents opportunity rather than mandatory progression.

Used for valuable upgrades, Eggs and future systems.

---

## Ore

Primary product created by mining.

Ore represents the player's work and serves as the foundation of the economy.

Different ores represent different progression stages.

---

## Pets

Long-term progression multipliers.

Pets should encourage different playstyles instead of acting as simple stat increases.

---

## Rebirth

Rebirth is not a currency.

It is a progression milestone that separates the game into distinct chapters and unlocks new content.

---

# Balance Principles

The economy follows the following design principles:

- Players should almost always be able to afford some form of progression.
- Major goals should always feel achievable.
- Grinding should never feel endless.
- Every session should produce visible progress.
- Every major purchase should unlock something meaningful.
- Older content becomes easier without becoming obsolete.
- New content should always feel rewarding.

---

# Economy Success Criteria

The economy is considered successful if:

- Players consistently feel progression.
- Every major purchase changes gameplay.
- Players always know their next objective.
- Players return because they are excited about future progression.
- The economy remains enjoyable during both early-game and late-game.

---

# OreLabs Economy Principle

> The economy should motivate players to discover something new rather than endlessly repeat something old.

This principle serves as the foundation for every future progression system in OreLabs.
