# OL-GD-006 Decisions

---

# Purpose

This document records all major design decisions made during the Game Discovery phase.

Its purpose is to preserve the reasoning behind important architectural choices and establish a single source of truth for the project's design philosophy.

Once a decision is marked as **Accepted**, it becomes part of the OreLabs design foundation and should only be changed through deliberate review.

---

# Decision Status

Possible decision states:

- Proposed
- Accepted
- Deprecated
- Replaced

Only **Accepted** decisions represent official game design.

# Accepted Decisions

---

## GD-DEC-001

### Title

Chapter-Based Progression

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

The game's progression is divided into chapters using the Rebirth system.

**Reason**

Players always understand where one stage ends and the next begins.

**Impact**

Rebirth becomes the primary long-term progression mechanic.

---

## GD-DEC-002

### Title

Strength Is the Foundation

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Strength is the root progression system of the entire game.

Every major gameplay system either depends on Strength or indirectly enhances it.

**Reason**

Creates one clear progression backbone.

**Impact**

Strength becomes the player's primary progression metric.

---

## GD-DEC-003

### Title

Pickaxes Unlock Content

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Pickaxes primarily unlock new ores, resources and areas instead of providing unlimited mining speed.

**Reason**

Progression should be based on exploration and advancement rather than raw numbers.

**Impact**

Every Pickaxe feels meaningful.

---

## GD-DEC-004

### Title

Continuous Mining Rewards

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Players receive ore progressively while mining instead of receiving everything only after destroying the resource.

**Reason**

Continuous feedback feels more rewarding.

**Impact**

Mining remains satisfying throughout the entire interaction.

---

## GD-DEC-005

### Title

Economy Prioritizes Progression

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

The economy is designed to create continuous progression rather than continuous grinding.

**Reason**

Players should return because progression feels exciting.

**Impact**

Every future economy system must support this philosophy.

---

## GD-DEC-006

### Title

Meaningful Purchases

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Every major purchase must unlock new gameplay opportunities instead of only increasing numbers.

**Reason**

Players remember new experiences more than larger values.

**Impact**

Future balancing should prioritize gameplay expansion.

---

## GD-DEC-007

### Title

Multiple Simultaneous Goals

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Players should always have short-term, mid-term and long-term goals at the same time.

**Reason**

Maintains motivation regardless of session length.

**Impact**

Every progression system must contribute to at least one goal horizon.

---

## GD-DEC-008

### Title

Old Content Never Becomes Obsolete

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Older content becomes easier as players progress but should never completely lose its purpose.

**Reason**

Creates a satisfying feeling of growth while preserving world consistency.

**Impact**

Progression accelerates naturally without invalidating previous gameplay.

---

## GD-DEC-009

### Title

Player Progress Is Measured by Possibilities

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Progress should be represented by new mechanics, new systems, new areas and new opportunities rather than simply larger numbers.

**Reason**

Unlocking possibilities creates stronger long-term engagement.

**Impact**

Future systems should prioritize expanding gameplay.

---

## GD-DEC-010

### Title

Progression Before Optimization

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

The player should first unlock new content and only later optimize efficiency.

**Reason**

Discovery should always precede optimization.

**Impact**

Gameplay remains fresh throughout progression.

---

## GD-DEC-011

### Title

Player Freedom Within Structure

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

The game should provide multiple progression paths while maintaining a clear overall progression structure.

**Reason**

Players should feel free without feeling lost.

**Impact**

Future systems should expand choices without breaking progression clarity.

---

## GD-DEC-012

### Title

Every Session Must Matter

**Status**

Accepted

**Decision**

Every gameplay session should move the player closer to at least one meaningful objective.

**Reason**

Players should never feel that their time was wasted.

**Impact**

Every future mechanic should contribute to visible progression.

---

# OreLabs Design Principle

> Every accepted decision exists to improve player experience, preserve progression clarity and support the long-term vision of the game.

When uncertainty appears during development, this document takes priority over assumptions.

Future decisions should extend this philosophy rather than contradict it.
