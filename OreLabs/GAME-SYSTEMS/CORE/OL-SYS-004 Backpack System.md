# OL-SYS-004 Backpack System

## Purpose

The Backpack System determines how many resources a player can carry before selling them.

It creates a natural gameplay rhythm between mining and selling.

The Backpack is not intended to slow progression unnecessarily.

It exists to create meaningful progression and improve long mining sessions.

---

## Core Philosophy

The Backpack controls the duration of each mining run.

A larger Backpack allows players to spend more time mining before returning to sell resources.

The Backpack should always feel like a quality-of-life upgrade rather than a punishment.

---

## Player Experience

The player should feel:

- I can stay in the mine longer.
- I don't need to return as often.
- My mining sessions are becoming more efficient.
- A larger Backpack makes progression feel smoother.

Every Backpack upgrade should immediately improve the gameplay experience.

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. Mine Resources.
2. Store Resources in the Backpack.
3. Reach Backpack capacity.
4. Sell all stored Resources.
5. Earn Coins.
6. Buy a larger Backpack.
7. Repeat.

---

## Backpack Capacity

Every Backpack has a maximum capacity.

Each mined Resource occupies Backpack space.

When the Backpack becomes full, the player must sell their Resources before continuing to mine.

---

## Progression

Each new Backpack increases carrying capacity.

Larger Backpacks allow:

- Longer mining sessions.
- Fewer selling trips.
- Better overall efficiency.
- Smoother progression.

Backpacks do not increase resource value.

They only increase carrying capacity.

---

## Relationship with Other Systems

Depends on:

- Mining System

Supports:

- Economy
- Ore System
- Strength progression
- Rebirth progression

---

## Rebirth Interaction

Future Backpack tiers may require specific Rebirth milestones.

This allows Backpack progression to remain synchronized with overall game progression.

Rebirth should unlock new Backpack tiers rather than simply increasing capacity.

---

## Design Rules

- Backpack upgrades must always feel useful.
- Capacity should grow steadily throughout progression.
- Backpack size should never become frustratingly small.
- Larger Backpacks improve convenience, not resource value.
- Backpack progression should remain simple and easy to understand.

---

## Future Extensions

Possible future additions:

- Backpack Skins
- Auto Sell
- Resource Filters
- Smart Storage
- Temporary Capacity Boosts
- Special Backpack Types

These additions should enhance convenience without replacing the core Backpack system.

---

## Success Criteria

The Backpack System is successful when:

- Players naturally alternate between mining and selling.
- Backpack upgrades noticeably improve gameplay flow.
- Selling never feels excessively frequent.
- Capacity remains balanced throughout progression.
- The Backpack supports long-term progression without unnecessary complexity.

---

## Backpack Principle

> A Backpack does not make resources more valuable.

> A Backpack allows the player to spend more time playing and less time interrupting their mining.
