# OL-CNT-005 Backpack Catalog

## Purpose

The Backpack Catalog defines all usable Backpacks in OreLabs.

Backpacks determine how many resources a player can carry before selling them.

The catalog exists to organize all Backpacks in a single structured system.

---

## Core Philosophy

Backpacks are progression tools.

They do not increase the value of resources.

They increase how long the player can stay active before needing to sell.

A better Backpack should feel like a quality-of-life upgrade and a progression milestone at the same time.

---

## Player Experience

The player should feel:

- I can stay in the mine longer.
- I do not need to return as often.
- My mining sessions feel smoother.
- A larger Backpack makes progression easier to manage.
- I want the next Backpack.

Every Backpack upgrade should immediately feel useful.

---

## Shop Structure

Backpacks are obtained through the shop system.

The shop may also contain other progression items such as:

- Pickaxes
- Future upgrades
- Future systems

When the player buys a Backpack, it becomes the active Backpack immediately.

The player may later re-equip previously unlocked Backpacks through the shop interface.

Backpacks do not need to be stored in a separate inventory system.

---

## Unlock Rules

Backpacks are primarily unlocked through progression.

Some Backpacks may also require:

- Coins
- Strength milestones
- Rebirth milestones
- Quests
- Special Events
- Secret Discovery
- Future systems

Strength remains one of the primary progression gates.

---

## Equip Rules

Buying a Backpack equips it immediately.

The player may manually equip any previously unlocked Backpack through the shop.

This keeps the system simple and easy to understand.

---

## Backpack Progression

Each Backpack represents a larger carrying capacity.

Larger Backpacks allow:

- Longer mining sessions
- Fewer selling trips
- Better overall efficiency
- Smoother progression flow

Backpacks should support progress without creating unnecessary friction.

---

## Relationship with Other Systems

Depends on:

- Mining System
- Strength System

Supports:

- Economy
- Ore System
- Rebirth
- Long-term Progression

---

## Design Rules

- Every Backpack must feel useful.
- Backpack upgrades should be simple to understand.
- Larger Backpacks should improve convenience.
- Backpacks should not increase resource value.
- The shop should allow players to inspect and re-equip unlocked Backpacks.
- Backpack progression should remain clean and scalable.

---

## Future Extensions

Possible future additions:

- Backpack Skins
- Auto Sell
- Resource Filters
- Smart Storage
- Special Backpack Types
- Temporary Capacity Boosts

These additions should improve convenience without replacing the core Backpack system.

---

## Success Criteria

The Backpack Catalog is successful when:

- Players naturally extend their mining sessions.
- Backpack upgrades feel meaningful.
- Selling resources feels balanced.
- Older Backpacks remain accessible.
- The system remains simple and scalable.

---

## Backpack Principle

> A Backpack does not make resources more valuable.

> A Backpack gives the player more time to play before selling.
