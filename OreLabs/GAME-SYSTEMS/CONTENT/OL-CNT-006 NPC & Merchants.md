# OL-CNT-006 NPC & Merchants

## Purpose

The NPC & Merchants System provides player interaction points throughout OreLabs.

NPCs guide progression, provide services, introduce new gameplay systems and make the world feel alive.

NPCs should always have a clear purpose.

---

## Core Philosophy

Every NPC exists for a reason.

Players should immediately understand what each NPC does.

NPCs are not decorations.

They are progression checkpoints, service providers and world-building characters.

---

## Player Experience

The player should feel:

- The world feels alive.
- Every NPC is useful.
- I always know where to go.
- Talking to NPCs is quick and intuitive.

NPC interaction should never interrupt gameplay.

---

# Main NPC Types

## Pickaxe Merchant

Functions:

- Buy Pickaxes
- Equip previously purchased Pickaxes
- View Strength requirements

The Pickaxe Merchant is located in the starting area.

---

## Backpack Merchant

Functions:

- Buy Backpacks
- Equip previously purchased Backpacks
- View Backpack capacities

The Backpack Merchant is located in the starting area.

---

## Ore Buyer

Functions:

- Sell collected resources
- Display total selling value

Every mining area may contain its own Ore Buyer.

Selling resources should always feel quick and convenient.

---

## Egg Station

Functions:

- Open Eggs
- Auto Open
- Auto Delete

Each Egg exists within its own World and acts as its own interaction point.

No additional NPC is required.

---

## Quest NPCs

Quest NPCs introduce:

- Story objectives
- Progression tasks
- Future events
- Hidden rewards

Quest NPCs should always be placed in logical locations within the world.

---

## Future NPCs

Possible future additions:

- Forge Master
- Blueprint Collector
- Material Researcher
- Event Merchant
- Rebirth Guide
- Seasonal NPCs
- Secret NPCs

---

# World Integration

The main progression shops remain in the starting area.

Players naturally return there throughout progression.

Mining areas primarily contain:

- Ore Buyers
- Quest NPCs
- Eggs
- Future progression NPCs

This keeps navigation simple while making the world feel populated.

---

# Interaction System

Players approach an NPC.

↓

Interaction prompt appears.

↓

The player interacts.

↓

A short dialogue appears.

↓

The corresponding interface opens.

The interaction should remain smooth and fast.

---

# Dialogue Philosophy

Every NPC should have short personality dialogue.

Examples:

Pickaxe Merchant

> "Looking for a stronger Pickaxe?"

Ore Buyer

> "Let's see what you've mined today."

Quest NPC

> "I could use your help, miner."

Forge Master

> "Let's create something extraordinary."

Dialogue should build personality without slowing gameplay.

---

# Visual Identity

Every NPC should have a recognizable appearance.

Players should immediately identify an NPC by:

- clothing
- tools
- animations
- location
- visual style

The player should recognize an NPC before reading its name.

# Design Rules

- Every NPC must have a clear purpose.
- Important NPCs should always be easy to find.
- Main shops remain in the starting area.
- Selling resources should always be convenient.
- Dialogue should be short and memorable.
- NPCs should make the world feel alive.
