# OL-CNT-002 World System

## Purpose

The World System defines how progression areas are structured in OreLabs.

Worlds represent the player's long-term journey through the game.

Each new World should feel like a meaningful step forward, not just a different background.

---

## Core Philosophy

Worlds divide the game into larger progression zones.

Players unlock new Worlds over time by progressing through the game.

Each World should contain new opportunities, new resources, and a new sense of discovery.

Worlds are not just locations.

Worlds are chapters of the player's adventure.

---

## Player Experience

The player should feel:

- I finally unlocked a new World.
- This place feels different.
- There are new things to discover here.
- I want to see what this World contains.

Each new World should feel exciting and rewarding.

---

## World Structure

OreLabs will use a staged World progression model.

Players begin in the first World and unlock later Worlds over time.

Each World may contain:

- Mining Areas
- Eggs
- NPCs
- Shops
- Future Systems
- Unique resources
- Unique visual themes

---

## World Progression

The first Worlds should feel accessible and easy to understand.

Later Worlds should become more advanced, more rewarding, and more visually distinct.

World progression should feel like gradual expansion.

---

## World Unlocking

Worlds may be unlocked through:

- Strength progression
- Pickaxe progression
- Rebirth milestones
- Game progression requirements
- Future systems

World access must always feel earned.

---

## World Economy

Some Worlds may have their own progression costs.

These costs should be balanced carefully so they support progression without making the game feel exhausting.

World access should create anticipation, not frustration.

---

## World Themes

The game may start with simple natural themes and gradually become more specialized.

Example progression:

1. Meadow
2. Forest
3. Beach
4. Winter
5. Cave

These are only example themes.

The final selection of Worlds may evolve later.

---

## World Content

Each World may contain its own content structure.

Examples:

- unique Ores
- unique Eggs
- unique Shops
- unique NPCs
- unique progress gates
- future exclusive systems

A World should feel like a complete gameplay zone.

---

## Monetization

World unlocks may support monetization in a limited way.

Examples:

- premium currency boosts
- temporary progression boosts
- faster access to later Worlds

Monetization should never invalidate normal gameplay progression.

Players should still be able to unlock Worlds through play.

---

## Relationship with Other Systems

Depends on:

- Strength System
- Pickaxe System
- Rebirth System
- Economy
- Egg System
- Future Systems

Supports:

- Progression pacing
- Exploration
- Replayability
- Content expansion

---

## Design Rules

- Every World must feel meaningful.
- Worlds should gradually expand the game.
- Later Worlds should feel more rewarding.
- World unlocks should be balanced carefully.
- The game should not feel like a simple list of disconnected maps.
- Worlds must support progression, not replace it.

---

## Future Extensions

Possible future additions:

- Special event Worlds
- Seasonal Worlds
- Hidden Worlds
- Rebirth-specific Worlds
- Premium shortcut access
- World-exclusive resources

---

## Success Criteria

The World System is successful when:

- players are excited to unlock the next World;
- each World feels meaningfully different;
- progression feels like traveling through a larger adventure;
- monetization does not break normal progression;
- Worlds support long-term retention.

---

## World Principle

> Worlds are chapters of the player's journey.

> Every new World should feel like a reward for progress.
