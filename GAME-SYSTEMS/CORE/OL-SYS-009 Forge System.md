# OL-SYS-009 Forge System

## Purpose

The Forge System is an advanced progression system unlocked during the middle or late stages of the game.

It allows players to customize equipment using resources collected throughout their journey.

The Forge transforms progression from linear upgrades into player-driven build creation.

---

## Core Philosophy

The Forge is not designed to create stronger equipment.

It is designed to let players create equipment that matches their preferred playstyle.

Every crafted item is a result of player decisions.

The Forge rewards planning, experimentation and long-term progression.

---

## Unlock Progression

The Forge is not available at the beginning of the game.

Players should first learn the core gameplay systems:

- Strength
- Mining
- Pickaxes
- Economy
- Pets
- Rebirth

Only after mastering the core progression should the Forge become available.

The Forge represents the second major stage of progression.

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. Unlock the Forge.
2. Discover a Blueprint.
3. Collect crafting materials.
4. Choose materials for each equipment part.
5. Assemble equipment.
6. Test the new build.
7. Improve and experiment with new combinations.

---

## Blueprints

Blueprints define the structure of the equipment.

Each Blueprint determines:

- Equipment type.
- Required parts.
- Maximum passive bonus capacity.
- Future crafting complexity.

Better Blueprints allow stronger and more advanced equipment builds.

Blueprints are permanent unlocks.

Possible sources include:

- Exploration
- Quests
- Events
- Bosses
- Future systems

---

## Materials

Every mineable resource may become a crafting material.

Each material provides its own passive effect.

Examples:

- Critical Chance
- Mining Speed
- Luck
- Gem Chance
- Mining Damage
- Strength Gain

Every material should have a clear identity.

---

## Passive Distribution

Blueprints define the maximum passive bonus available.

If all equipment parts are crafted from the same material, the entire passive bonus is applied to that single effect.

Example:

Emerald Handle

+

Emerald Head

↓

+10% Luck

If different materials are used, the available passive bonus is distributed between them.

Example:

Ruby Handle

+

Diamond Head

↓

+5% Critical Chance

+5% Mining Speed

Players choose between specialization and versatility.

---

## Advanced Materials

Early materials provide simple positive effects.

Later materials may introduce both advantages and disadvantages.

Examples:

+ Mining Speed

- Strength Gain

or

+ Critical Chance

- Gem Chance

These trade-offs create meaningful build decisions.

Negative effects should encourage different playstyles rather than punish the player.

---

## Equipment Customization

Initially the Forge supports:

- Pickaxes

Future updates may introduce:

- Training Equipment
- Backpacks
- Accessories
- Cosmetic Equipment

The system should naturally expand over time.

---

## Relationship with Other Systems

Depends on:

- Mining System
- Pickaxe System
- Ore System
- Rebirth System

Supports:

- Long-term Progression
- Build Diversity
- Economy
- Exploration
- Endgame Content

---

## Design Rules

- Every material must have value.
- Every Blueprint must feel rewarding.
- Players should experiment with different builds.
- There should rarely be one perfect combination.
- Build diversity is more important than raw power.
- The Forge should never become mandatory during early progression.

---

## Future Extensions

Possible future additions:

- Material Fusion
- Rare Materials
- Secret Blueprints
- Additional Equipment Types
- Cosmetic Forging
- Legendary Crafting Projects

---

## Success Criteria

The Forge System is successful when:

- Players enjoy experimenting with builds.
- Different crafting strategies remain viable.
- Older materials remain useful.
- Blueprints encourage exploration.
- The Forge becomes a meaningful long-term progression system.

---

## Forge Principle

> Blueprints define the structure.

> Materials define the passive abilities.

> Player choices define the final build.
