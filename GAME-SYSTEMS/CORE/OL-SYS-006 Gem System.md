# OL-SYS-006 Gem System

## Purpose

The Gem System introduces a secondary progression currency.

Gems are significantly rarer than Coins and represent valuable opportunities rather than basic progression.

Players should always be excited when Gems appear.

---

## Core Philosophy

Coins drive everyday progression.

Gems drive strategic progression.

Gems should never completely replace Coins.

Instead, they unlock systems that enhance long-term gameplay.

---

## Player Experience

The player should feel:

- I got lucky.
- Gems always feel valuable.
- I finally have enough Gems.
- I can unlock something important.

Receiving Gems should always be exciting.

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. Mine Ores.
2. Occasionally receive Gems.
3. Collect Gems.
4. Spend Gems on valuable upgrades.
5. Improve long-term progression.
6. Return to mining.

---

## Gem Sources

Primary source:

- Mining Ores

Future sources may include:

- Events
- Rare Ores
- Quests
- Achievements
- Future game systems

Mining should always remain the primary source of Gems.

---

## Gem Usage

Gems may be used for:

- Eggs
- Permanent Upgrades
- Future Systems
- Special Shop Items
- Quality-of-Life features

Gems should rarely be spent on basic progression.

---

## Relationship with Other Systems

Depends on:

- Mining System
- Ore System

Supports:

- Pet System
- Upgrade System
- Economy
- Future Content

---

## Design Rules

- Gems must remain valuable.
- Gems must remain relatively rare.
- Players should never feel forced to buy Gems.
- Spending Gems should always feel meaningful.
- Gems should reward active gameplay.

---

## Future Extensions

Possible future additions:

- Gem Multipliers
- Gem Events
- Daily Gem Rewards
- Premium Gem Shop
- Limited-Time Offers

These additions must preserve the value of Gems.

---

## Success Criteria

The Gem System is successful when:

- Players become excited whenever Gems drop.
- Gems remain valuable throughout the game.
- Spending Gems always feels rewarding.
- Gems support progression without replacing Coins.

---

## Gem Principle

> Gems are not a replacement for Coins.

> Gems represent rare opportunities that accelerate or enrich progression without becoming mandatory.
